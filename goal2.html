Goal 2

Understand the essential nature of information and its relevance to society.

Outcome 2a. Describe various definitions and concepts of information.

  • Artifact: Metadata definitions
  • Class: LIS 882- Metadata for Digital Resources

While most of my time in the MLIS program has dealt with digital archives, the backbone to managing information is understanding and accurately creating metadata and is similar for analog and digital records. In this discussion post I provide summaries of four different definitions of metadata from the week’s readings and the outcomes if the different interpretations were applied to the same information. As an information professional, it is important to apply the same processes to similar materials in order to improve searchability for the user while ensuring trust in the information. However, it is also important to evaluate each record or collection for their individual qualities and determine when the metadata should deviate.

Outcome 2b. Explain the impact of information policies on intellectual freedom, access, literacy, information behavior, and other aspects of library and information science.

  • Artifact: Research Paper on Copyright Law for Creative Works
  • Class: LIS 701- Core Values, Ethics, and Issues in the Library and Information Professions

For my LIS 701 research paper, I explored how copyright law has historically impacted creativity and design, and the alternatives that are emerging. As a life-long artist, I think it is important to understand how to protect your art from others at the same time as avoiding copyright infringement of other’s works. By learning about the history of copyright and how current technologies are impacting our laws to protect the publishers at the expense of the creators, it is important to guide users to comprehensive information so they can continue to use and benefit from their works into the future.

Outcome 2c. Develop appropriate responses to assessed information needs within diverse communities/organizations.

  • Artifact: Equality Podcast audio file
    Equality Podcast script
  • Class: LIS 702- Facilitating User Learning and Information Needs

My classes in my first semester included several early discussions about how to include diverse communities in a library. Consequently, when I was given the opportunity, I took advantage of submitting an essay in a non-traditional format. I used this opportunity to learn how to record audio narration on a computer since many users may not read or understand English well.

This diversity essay was written and presented as a podcast. It highlights how various areas of a person’s identity are interwoven to make up their intersectionality. I point out that librarians and, more broadly, information professions, may unconsciously rely on their own experiences to select materials and collections. To break those invisible privilege barriers and create a welcoming environment, we need to assess how different groups within a local community may view incorrect stereotypes or missing identities, and then consciously shift towards diversifying our collections and information.
