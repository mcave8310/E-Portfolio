Goal 2

Understand the essential nature of information and its relevance to society.

Outcome 2a. Describe various definitions and concepts of information.

  • Artifact: Metadata Definitions Discussion Post
  • Class: LIS 882- Metadata for Digital Resources

While most of my time in the MLIS program has dealt with digital archives, the backbone to managing information is understanding and accurately creating metadata, and is similar for analog and digital records. In this discussion post I provide summaries of four different definitions of metadata from the week’s readings, and also the outcomes if the different interpretations were applied to the same information. As an information professional, it is important to be aware of various definitions and concepts of information in order to understand how to apply them to different situations. It is often most useful to apply the same processes to similar materials in order to improve user searchability while ensuring trust in accuracy and validity of the information. However, care has to be taken to evaluate each record or collection for their individual qualities and determine when the metadata should deviate.

Outcome 2b. Explain the impact of information policies on intellectual freedom, access, literacy, information behavior, and other aspects of library and information science.

  • Artifact: Research Paper on Copyright Law
  • Class: LIS 701- Core Values, Ethics, and Issues in the Library and Information Professions

For my LIS 701 research paper, I explored how copyright law has historically impacted creativity and design, as well as the permission alternatives that are emerging as technology advances. By learning about the history of copyright and how current technologies are impacting our laws, it is important to guide users to comprehensive information so they can continue to use and benefit from their works in the future. As information professionals, we may have a responsibility to explain the impact of information policies on their intellectual freedom, access, and other aspects of their lives. In the case of copyright law, many users unwittingly, or otherwise, depend on Fair Use and may need some instruction to better understand the consequences of their actions. Similarly, we need to understand how those laws and regulations directly impact or target library and information science areas, and how to appeal when they become too restrictive or fail to keep up with technology changes.

Outcome 2c. Develop appropriate responses to assessed information needs within diverse communities/organizations.

  • Artifact: Equality Podcast audio file
    Equality Podcast script
  • Class: LIS 702- Facilitating User Learning and Information Needs

My classes in my first semester included several early discussions about how to include diverse communities in a library. Consequently, when I was given the opportunity, I took advantage of submitting an essay in a non-traditional format. I used this opportunity to learn how to record audio narration on a computer by creating a podcast since many users may not read English well.

As an information professional, it is important to develop appropriate responses to assessed information needs within diverse communities. This diversity essay highlights how various areas of a person’s identity are interwoven to make up their intersectionality and that librarians need to be aware of the seen a nd hidden diversity within their users. By developing appropriate responses as information needs are assessed, I will have a sense of how to react to situations quicker involving diverse communities. This subsequently would ideally build trust and create open communication.
