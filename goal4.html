Goal 4

Synthesize theory and practice within a dynamic, evolving, and diverse information environment.

Outcome 4a. Apply library and information science theories, principles, and research to professional practice.

  • Artifact: RIM Program Plan
  • Class: LIS 886- Records and Information Management

Archivists and libraries have much in common with businesses and often rely upon Records and Information Management (RIM) plans. The final project for LIS 886 was to create a RIM program plan for a fictional university that was similar to Dominican University. I was expected to distinguish between urgent and long-term problems as well as address the needs of the program at thirty days, ninety days, and one year. This plan incorporated laws and policy standards for PII (personally identifiable information), access controls, retention schedules, and digital security. Further, I also addressed the need to incorporate the Archives Department when creating the university retention schedule and disposition plan, and necessity of maintaining a disaster response plan.

Outcome 4b. Evaluate current and emerging technologies for information capture, organization, dissemination, access, and/or curation to meet the variety of information needs of diverse communities.

  • Artifact: Linked Data Discussion Post
  • Class: LIS 882- Metadata for Digital Resources

This discussion post provides an overview of linked data and its benefits. As an emerging technology, it has yet to be embraced by the wider library and archival community. Incorporating linked data into an existing catalog would improve searchability with machine-readability content by instilling controlled vocabularies and fixing broken links between hyperlinks and webpages.

Outcome 4c. Demonstrate teaching and learning principles in relation to professional practice.

  • Artifact: LibGuide about Book Arts
  • Class: LIS 702- Facilitating User Learning and Information Needs

In LIS 702 we were asked to teach something and create an accompanying LibGuide. For my lesson, I taught how to fold an accordion book from one sheet of paper. My LibGuide included the instruction sheet from my lesson that I drew by hand, but went further and explored other methods and styles of bookbinding for all ages. As information professionals it is important to relate a project or search result to the individual user. My lesson and LibGuide were intended for beginners of all ages and emphasized supplies commonly found around a house. While I think that it is valuable to teach the history of books and of bookbinding, it is more important to relate it to modern times and showing a range of design options and resources. Not only does that demonstrate relevance of the library to the community but it creates an inviting environment.
