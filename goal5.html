Goal 5

Effectively communicate and collaborate to deliver, market, and advocate for library and information services.

Outcome 5a. Apply marketing and/or advocacy principles to demonstrate and promote the value of libraries and information agencies.

  • Artifact: Marketing Discussion Post
  • Class: LIS 707- Leadership, Marketing, and Strategic Communication

In this marketing discussion post, I looked at a service offered by the Cuyahoga County Public Library (CCPL) near Cleveland, OH and analyzed the benefits and concerns of the Jack, Joseph and Morton Mandel Memory Lab. The ability to digitize family records is becoming easier and more affordable at the same time as old family records, such as photos, slides, and video, are aging. By offering and marketing this service to the community, CCPL demonstrates its relevancy and value.

Outcome 5b. Demonstrate effective professional communication to achieve common understanding as an individual or in group settings.

  • Artifact: Research Question Group Assignment
  • Class: LIS 708- Evidence-Based Planning, Management, and Decision Making

The final research project in LIS 708 was a mixture of group and individual work. As a group we had to narrow our research topic from an assigned broad topic, share our research with the group, and then together create research questions and a survey for our classmates to fill out. We met weekly to discuss our findings and help each other understand complex or confusing ideas. In this group assignment, each group member was responsible for a section of the research question write-up. Then we came together on Zoom and merged everyone’s writings in a Google Doc.

Outcome 5c. Demonstrate leadership principles in an educational, professional, or community context.

  • Artifact: Leadership Research Paper
  • Class: LIS 707- Leadership, Marketing, and Strategic Communication

My leadership research paper for LIS 707 scrutinized how women in library fields can use their influence and natural tendencies to receive career promotions. I combined my learning from the book The Influence Effect: A New Path to Power for Women Leaders and interviews with two librarians from the Library of Congress, which is arguably of the most bureaucratic and business-like libraries, to understand that successful leadership for women includes strategically creating relationships and trust at different levels, envisioning future outcomes and responses, building a network of support, and marketing yourself and your ideas towards your envisioned goals.
