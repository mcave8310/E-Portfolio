Goal 3

Navigate, curate and create information across the spectrum of human records.

Outcome 3a. Design resources or tools that facilitate access to information.

  • Artifact: Sr. Suzanne Noffke Papers EAD3 XML
  • Class: LIS 799- Practicum, and Student Volunteer in the McGreal Center

In my practicum I asked to work on two different projects, a digital project and an analog collection. As previously mentioned, the digital project was the Sacred Spaces Architecture Project. The analog collection I worked on was the Sr. Suzanne Noffke papers. This was a newly acquired collection of Sr. Noffke’s research about and translations of St. Catherine of Siena. It was my responsibility to assess, arrange, inventory, and create a finding aid in ArchivesSpace. Since most of my time with the collection dealt with digitizing the multimedia (ie. powerpoint files, floppy discs, homemade DVDs and CDs, and microfilm) due to their deterioration, I returned as a volunteer to continue inputting the paper folders into ArchivesSpace.

In order to understand the contents and the significance of the collection, I spend several early hours researching Sr. Noffke, St. Catherine and St. Dominic. That research not only was included in the Finding Aid but helped me as I assessed and arranged the disorganized boxes.

Outcome 3b. Utilize resources or tools to manage information.

  • Artifact: Omeka Collection about Theatre during Pandemics
  • Class: LIS 889- Digital Curation

In LIS 889, I curated a collection of news articles, websites, podcasts and YouTube videos about how the theatre community has adapted to and overcome the challenges to performing during pandemics. Using the open source web-publishing platform Omeka, I was able to store my digital collection, add metadata and files, and create a digital exhibit. Using this software highlighted how users are reliant on quality navigation. I found it challenging to move around both my collection and exhibit, and had to think diligently about how users would view and access the information. Since this is a born-digital collection, the quality of my metadata played a large role in understanding and viewing the items.

Outcome 3c. Evaluate resources or tools that manage and facilitate access to information.

  • Artifact: Comparing EAD3 to EAD2002
  • Class: LIS 881- Advanced Archives

An EAD finding aid is an archival standard when working with collections. With the advancement of technology and the gaining prevalence of born-digital collections, EAD3 is the latest version that includes linked data, EAC-CPF, international standards, and multilingual records. In Advanced Archives I compared the five main areas that changed between EAD 2002 and EAD3.
