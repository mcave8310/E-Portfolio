Goal 3

Navigate, curate and create information across the spectrum of human records.

Outcome 3a. Design resources or tools that facilitate access to information.

  • Artifact: This E-Portfolio
  • Class: LIS 753- Internet Fundamentals

For my final project in LIS 753, I coded this e-Portfolio. It is important when designing resources and tools that facilitate access to information, especially for online usage, that they are designed to meet a purpose, are appropriate for the intended user, and incorporate responsive design for diverse types of devices. This e-Portfolio was coded and validated to be visually pleasing to the average user while also accounting for accessibility users who depend on alternative text and colors. While my portfolio is best viewed on a laptop or desktop monitor, there is some responsive design for viewing on a small device such as a tablet. Each individual page is also titled so that it clearly states the information that it contains. Further, the navigation bar at the top is repeated at the bottom so that the user does not have to scroll back up the top in order to visit another page.

Outcome 3b. Utilize resources or tools to manage information.

  • Artifact: Omeka Collection: Theatre in the Time of a Pandemic
  • Class: LIS 889- Digital Curation

In LIS 889, I curated a collection of news articles, websites, podcasts and YouTube videos about how the theatre community has adapted to and overcome the challenges to performing during pandemics. Using the open source web-publishing platform Omeka, I was able to store my digital collection, add metadata and files, and create a digital exhibit. While using this software, I became aware of how users are reliant on quality navigation. I found it challenging to move around both my collection and exhibit, and had to think diligently about how users would view and access the information. Since this is a born-digital collection, the quality of my metadata played a large role in understanding and viewing the items. Like with many other digital projects, I have found that it is important how I am familiar with the software so that I can utilize its features to produce a better product. Despite using an older version of Omeka in class, this project allowed me to develop experience with the basic software layout and operation of actions.

Outcome 3c. Evaluate resources or tools that manage and facilitate access to information.

  • Artifact: Comparing EAD3 to EAD2002 Report
  • Class: LIS 881- Advanced Archives

When evaluating resources or tools that manage and facilitate access to information, it is important to not only assess the purpose and desired results of the goal, but also how well we are will be able to utilize the tool. This includes information and archival standards such as EAD. In this report from LIS 881, I compared the five main areas that changed between EAD 2002 and EAD3. It is not only important to understand a standard’s original guidelines, but as information professionals, we need to keep up with the how and why of changes as new versions of standards are published. This, in turn, will also allow us remain relevant and acquainted with technology improvements and terminology changes.
